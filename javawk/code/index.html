Husky

The Siberian Husky is a friendly and energetic breed. Originally bred as sled dogs their energetic yet gentle nature also makes them the perfect fit for family life, given they still have enough space to run around.

Is the Siberian Husky the right dog for you?

Husky Traits
Social Affectionate Playfull Good w/ kids and dogs
Physical High shedding Moderate grooming Low drooling
Personality High energy Frequent barking Moderate to train

Husky Attributes

  • Height 21-23.5 inches (male) and 20-22 inches (female)
  • Weight 45-60 pounds (male) and 35-50 pounds (female)
  • Life Expectancy from 12-14 years

Samoyed At First Glance

Shiba Inu

The Shiba Inu is a cute, proud, and stubborn breed of dog. They are a hunting dog originating in Japan and are currently the number one most beloved dog breed there. Although the Shiba Inu is prized for its beautiful foxlike appearance and thick orange to tan coat, they come in other colors such as black, cream and sesame. For additional impromation please check the the Shiba Inu American Kennel Club website.

Is the Shiba Inu your perfect dog?

What you should expect
Trainability Intelegent Difficult to train Can be stubborn
Family Dog Indapendent Good with older children Not Hypoallergenic
Grooming and Care 1 hour of daily exercise Weekly brushing Feed daily with high quality dog food

Shiba Inu Attributes

  • Height from 14.5-16.5 inches (male) and 13.5-15.5 inches (female)
  • Weight from 23 pounds (male) and 17 pounds (female)
  • Lifespan from 13-16 years

Where to get your Shiba Inu

  1. Shiba Inus can be purchase from a responsible breeder.
  2. Shiba Inus can be found in animal shelters or pounds.

Where NOT to get your Shiba Inu

  1. You should NOT purchase a Shiba Inu from a pet store or puppy mill.
  2. Be careful what online sites you buy your puppy from; they may also be from a puppy mill. Make sure to always meet the parents of your new puppy before purchase.

Chow Chow

The Chow Chow is a dog breed originally from northern China. The Chow Chow is a sturdily built dog, square in profile, with a broad skull and small, triangular, erect ears with rounded tips. The breed is known for a very dense double coat that is either smooth or rough. For more information visit The American Kennel Club.
